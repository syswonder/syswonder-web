# Charter of the Syswonder Ubiquitous Operating System Open Source Community

## Article 1 (Name)

The name of this community is the **Syswonder Ubiquitous Operating System Open Source Community**  
(hereinafter referred to as the **“Syswonder Community”**).

---

## Article 2 (Mission)

The Syswonder Community is a **non-profit, vendor-neutral open source technical community** dedicated to the research and development of key technologies for next-generation ubiquitous operating systems targeting future ubiquitous intelligent application scenarios, including but not limited to embodied intelligence, intelligent manufacturing, and other in-situ computing environments.

The Community aims to collaboratively build advanced open source projects with international impact. It encourages researchers and engineering professionals from different organizations to engage in **open, collaborative research and innovation** through the joint development of open source projects.

---

## Article 3 (Intellectual Property)

All outputs produced within the Community’s open source projects, including but not limited to project names, branding, source code, documentation, and technical solutions, shall be governed and maintained by the Community in accordance with the applicable open source licenses and the provisions of this Charter.

The Community supports its members in lawfully conducting scientific research projects, technical research, and industrial applications based on Community open source projects, and encourages contributors to continuously contribute derivative technologies and improvements back to the Community.

When submitting code, documentation, or other contributions to Community projects, contributors represent that they either own the intellectual property rights to their contributions or have obtained sufficient and lawful authorization to submit such contributions and license them to the Community.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, contributors, by submitting a contribution, are deemed to grant the Community and its users a **perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, irrevocable, and non-exclusive license**, without transferring copyright ownership, to use, reproduce, modify, publish, distribute, and sublicense such contributions under the terms of the applicable open source licenses adopted by the Community.

---

## Article 4 (Technical Committee)

The Community establishes a **Technical Committee** (hereinafter referred to as the **“TC”**), which is responsible for defining the technical direction of the Community, managing Community open source projects, and overseeing related technical matters.

The Technical Committee operates under a democratic and consultative decision-making process. Resolutions shall be adopted upon approval by a **simple majority of all TC members**.

The Technical Committee shall democratically elect one **Chair** for a term of three (3) years. The election of the Chair requires approval by **at least two-thirds (2/3) of all existing TC members**.

The responsibilities of the Chair include:

- Formulating and organizing the implementation of the Technical Committee’s work plans;
- Convening and presiding over Technical Committee meetings;
- Promoting the technical development of Community projects;
- Handling other matters related to technology and project development.

Additional Technical Committee members may be appointed as required by the development of the Community and its projects. The appointment of new members requires approval by **at least two-thirds (2/3) of all existing TC members**.

---

## Article 5 (Open Source Community Committee)

The Community establishes an **Open Source Community Committee**, which is responsible for promoting open source development, ecosystem building, and application outreach, expanding the influence of Community projects, and supporting the sustainable operation of the Community.

The Open Source Community Committee shall have one **Chair**, nominated by the Technical Committee and approved by **at least two-thirds (2/3) of all existing Technical Committee members**, for a term of three (3) years.

The responsibilities of the Chair include:

- Formulating and organizing the implementation of the Community Committee’s work plans;
- Convening and presiding over Community Committee meetings;
- Promoting community operations, membership development, and partner engagement.

---

## Article 6 (Community Administration)

The Community establishes one or more **Community Administrators**, responsible for daily operations, infrastructure maintenance, and related support activities. Community Administrators shall be nominated by the Technical Committee.

---

## Article 7 (Disclaimer)

All open source project outputs and related materials released by the Community are provided on an **“AS IS”** basis.

The Community, its Technical Committee, Open Source Community Committee members, Community Administrators, and contributors make **no express or implied warranties**, including but not limited to warranties of merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, accuracy, or reliability.

Any organization or individual assumes all risks and consequences arising from the use, modification, or distribution of Community outputs. To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, the Community shall not be liable for any damages arising therefrom.

---

## Article 8 (Interpretation)

The right of interpretation of this Charter shall vest in the **Technical Committee of the Syswonder Ubiquitous Operating System Open Source Community**.

---

This Charter shall enter into force as of the date of its publication.
